John Tyler McCarley

Product Designer // Product Manager

Design is not just a job or even a way of thinking; it is a lifestyle. You can't put your design hat on during work hours only to surrender it when 5 O'Clock rolls around. A good designer arranges his life. Every space, every touchpoint, every interaction must fit the designer's framework.

And this is precisely the problem with tech: it has no taste. It lacks that je ne se quois, that unpalpable element, that invisible quality that gives life to a productâ€” that which we call art. It has no taste because the majority of people who design for tech wear unappealing clothes, eat bland food, and live in indistinguishable abodes. It is no wonder that companies struggle to produce fresh ideas and truly innovative products. This is where I part ways with the industry at large. Tech is not my life, and UX/UI Design is not all I care about. And that is precisely what makes me a great designer.
